# Firm Model Plan

## Scope

- v0 geography: Colombo District only.
- Unit split: firms and buying centers are separate but linked through `firm_id`.

## Firm Size Bands

- micro
- small
- medium

These bands are modeled separately because budget posture, risk tolerance, procurement style, and support burden differ sharply across them.

## Sector Layer

- Prompt #01 initial focus sectors:
  - services and BPO
  - retail and trade
  - logistics-enabled operations
  - professional services
- `INFERENCE`: those sectors are the most practical pilot targets because they are urban, workflow-rich, and likely to encounter externally sold AI tools.

## Digital Maturity

- nascent
- emerging
- operational
- advanced

Digital maturity controls likely integration capacity, data readiness, and the need for managed support.

## Budget Posture

- constrained
- targeted
- expansionary

Budget posture should remain separate from size-band because crisis conditions and sector economics can compress spending even inside larger firms.

## Buying Triggers

- service backlog
- labor shortage
- compliance pressure
- revenue growth push
- cost-reduction pressure

## Decision-Maker Roles

- economic buyer
- technical buyer
- user champion
- approver

Prompt #01 uses simplified buying-center templates rather than simulating internal politics in detail.

## Adoption Friction

- low
- medium
- high

Friction should reflect:
- data readiness
- workflow disruption risk
- vendor trust
- support burden
- procurement complexity

## Formal vs Informal Procurement Behavior

- formal firms are more likely to show committee or manager-led approvals.
- informal or owner-led firms are more likely to use fast, relationship-driven procurement.
- `TBD`: exact proportions and pathways require later calibration.
